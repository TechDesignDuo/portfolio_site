Trial By Fire

Working in kitchens has been the most significant part of my development as an adult. A proper kitchen is a place of learning, discipline, practice and gratification. That being said, it has been a rocky journey. Many moments of uncertainty in myself and my abilities; but I never let that stop me. The result is a massive amount of important work-skills and memories...

The Pandemic

Ever heard of COVID? Yeah, I know you have. The most rocky part of my entire life. As restaurant workers, my life partner and I were both laid off on March 17th, 2020 (and believe me, I didn't need to double-check that date). But I was lucky. The tough-loving kitchen life prepared me for this challenge physically, and my partner helped me mentally. This date marks the beginning of an "idea" to learn new skills while waiting to go back to work.

TechDesignDuo

From the ashes of my last three years, rose a new idea: what if I took my favourite parts of my cooking career - strategizing, designing, and creating - and applied it to something else? The world of tech is loaded with all sorts of possibilities. I wasn't sure which path to take, so I took some advice and "tried out" Python... Coolest thing ever. From there, it's been a journey of trying, failing and learning. My partner helped me create the TechDesignDuo logo (she completes the Duo in my adventure) and so here we are today.

Staying Productive in Lockdown

The majority of my focus for the last year has revolved around studying Python. However, I was also hired for a few paid projects along the way. I would like to briefly introduce you to one of my personal projects, as well as two projects that I was hired for.

In The Loop

Behold, my biggest project of the year: "In The Loop". I put together a program that gathers data across three different APIs, which then gets used for creating graphs, tables and other useful info. It is designed as a tool for a quick reference for anyone who is curious about a company and its stock performance at a glance.

Check it out on Github

Working With Locals

The biggest lesson that I learned in my culinary career is to respect the hard work of small, family-owned businesses. At my last restaurant, we sourced 99% of our products from local farmers, located within 100km from our position. Naturally, I was excited to try my hand at doing web development and design work with local businesses when the opportunity arose.

Working With Project-Movers

A local, family-owned business, Project-Movers is a moving company that specializes in both residential and commercial moves. The owner, a close friend of mine, asked me for my help this year to update their site. We rebuilt everything. The only original items are now the logo and the domain name. But we didn't stop there. Since then, we also applied months of work to improve their online presence. Thanks to our team effort, their website is now helping them generate a steady profit. If you're curious to hear more about Project-Movers and our conquest over Google Ads, be sure to drop me a message.

Visit Project-Movers!

Working With Clémex

Confiserie Clémex is a family-owned candy business. They specialize in high quality candy-in-bulk, as well as candy art. I used to run the Instagram page at my last restaurant and gained a fast hand at photo edits. Naturally, when they offered me candy as payment for occasional touch-ups of their art, I was more than happy to help!

Love candy? Visit them!
